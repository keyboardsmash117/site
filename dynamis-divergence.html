Dynamis Divergence

Instanced high-difficulty Dynamis

Dynamis Divergence is the Seekers-era re-imagining of classic Dynamis — fully instanced, much harder than original Dynamis, with its own custom mob pools and drop tables. On Scythe, all four "Divergence" zones are available at retail-faithful difficulty, tuned for a coordinated group (alliance-size recommended for the original nation zones, full party for Jeuno).

How to start
  1. Complete Rhapsodies of Vana'diel. Divergence entry is gated behind A Rhapsody for the Ages (final RoV mission). See the Missions page; mission skip is available from the hub NPC if you want to jump to endgame.
  2. Gather a currency piece. You need one of: One Hundred Byne Bill, Montiont Silverpiece, or Lungo-Nango Jadeshell (standard Dynamis currency — drops from original Dynamis, bought on the AH, or traded from guildmates).
  3. Visit Aurix. Aurix · Ru'Lude Gardens Trade your currency piece. In return you receive an Empty Hourglass key item — this is your Divergence pass.
  4. Buy a Prismatic Hourglass. 50,000 gil at the in-city Enigmatic Footprints NPCs (Windurst Walls, Bastok Mines, Southern San d'Oria, or Ru'Lude Gardens) — this converts the empty hourglass into a timed entry.
  5. Talk to the same Enigmatic Footprints NPC to enter the Divergence instance of your choice. Your alliance enters with you.
  6. Lockout: 24 hours from midnight. Standard Dynamis lockout. One run per player per day.

What Makes Divergence Different

  • Custom mob pools — not just up-levelled original Dynamis. Divergence mobs have different AI, different TP moves, and different loot.
  • Disjoined beastman champions — each zone has its own mini-bosses (Disjoineds_Galka, Disjoineds_Elvaan, Disjoineds_Mithra, Disjoineds_Tarutaru) with unique mechanics.
  • Elemental Circles — elemental mobs (Fire Circle, Ice Circle, etc.) drop crafting and upgrade materials.
  • Jeuno's Disjoineds_Odin — the final boss of Jeuno Divergence. Drops the highest-tier currency variant.
  • Retail-faithful droplists — the Divergence drop tables match what the Seekers-era retail server actually had.
