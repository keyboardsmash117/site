FATE

Open-world dynamic events across the leveling path — 78 FATEs in 38 zones

Overview

FATEs are open-world timed events modeled on the system of the same name from another well-known online Final Fantasy. While you are exploring, an event can erupt in your current zone — a sudden swarm of enemies or a powerful boss appears in a defined area. Everyone in the zone is invited, everyone shares the rewards, and your contribution is scored individually.

Scythe ships 78 FATEs across 38 outdoor zones covering the full 1-99 leveling path, with extras in the Abyssea, Adoulin, and Escha endgame zones. Each zone has its own level cap — when you engage a FATE above your level, you are synced down so the encounter scales properly.

How to participate
  1. Travel to any FATE-enabled zone. A FATE has a chance to spawn whenever the in-game hour changes, provided the zone's per-FATE cooldown is up (annihilation FATEs reset in 40 minutes, notorious FATEs in up to 2 hours).
  2. Watch zone chat for the [FATE] announcement — it names the event, the landmark/map grid, and the time limit.
  3. Go to the landmark and find the FATE Sync NPC at the center of the event circle. Talk to it to apply the level sync.
  4. FATE mobs are untargetable until at least one synced player engages — this prevents stealth-pulls before parties form. Once unlocked, the mobs activate for everyone.
  5. Fight. Your kills and your time inside the FATE radius (default 60 yalms) both count toward your contribution score.
  6. When the FATE completes, rewards are distributed automatically based on your individual score — no looting, no party setup required.

Contribution & Rewards

Your contribution is scored from two sources: kill credit (10 per trash kill, 120 per notorious boss) and time in the FATE radius (1 point per second). The total determines your tier — Gold, Silver, or Bronze.

Pets and trusts credit their master. The killer's full party is added to the contribution list automatically, so traditional XP party play works as expected. Enmity-list participants get credit too, so support roles are not shut out.

EXP scaling: The percentage is taken from the EXP required to reach the level cap of the zone you're in. So a Gold finish in a level-85 Abyssea FATE awards ~9,800 raw EXP, while a Gold finish in a level-99 Adoulin FATE awards ~11,200 raw EXP. The map.EXP_RATE multiplier (4× on Scythe) is then applied on top by the server.

FATE Types

Every FATE is one of two types. The announcement names the event but does not tell you which type — you'll see at a glance: a crowd of enemies means annihilation; one large mob means notorious.

Annihilation

Kill a target number of mobs (10-24 depending on the zone's level band) inside the time limit. Mobs spawn in two waves — when the first wave is roughly half cleared, reinforcements arrive with additional spawns, raising your kill target. Each mob has 3× HP and 2× attack/defense over its native pool.

Notorious

A single boss spawn. The boss has 10-15× HP, full stat scaling (5-8× combat stats), and uses the family's native mob skills. Defeat the boss before the timer expires to clear. These are tuned for a coordinated party at the listed level cap — solo clears require gear and class advantage.

Tips

  • Always sync first. Talk to the FATE Sync NPC before attacking — if you try to engage without sync, the mob resets and disengages.
  • Trusts work, but… If a trust starts engaging before you've synced, the mobs may stay locked. Always touch the Sync NPC yourself.
  • Stay inside the radius. The tick loop credits 5 seconds of "time score" every 5 real seconds you remain inside the FATE circle. Stepping out stops your time accrual but doesn't lose existing contribution.
  • Pets and trusts credit their master. BST charm pets, SMN avatars, and trust kills all count for you. So do enmity-list participants — support roles share in the reward.
  • Cooldowns are per-FATE. One zone can have 4 FATEs (Pashhow, Sauromugue) that rotate, so a zone with active players will usually have something to do within 10-15 minutes.
  • Solo Bronze is fine. Even minimal participation yields 1 Lumina and a small EXP boost — don't skip a FATE just because you're alone.
  • Level-restrict pre-existing. If you already have a LEVEL_RESTRICTION effect from another source, FATE sync won't override it — the FATE message will tell you to clear it first.
