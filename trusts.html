Trust Guide

Your NPC party members, explained

What Are Trusts?

Trusts are NPC companions you can summon to fight alongside you. They fill party roles just like real players — tanking, healing, dealing damage, and casting buffs. You can have up to 5 trusts active at once (filling a full party of 6 with you).

Each trust has a specific job and role. Some trusts have upgraded II versions that are stronger or behave differently. Building a good trust party is the key to soloing most content in FFXI.

Scythe has 122 trusts total. Many come from story missions, nation quests, and Records of Eminence. Others can be purchased with Login Points from the Greeter Moogle. This guide covers every single one.

Quick Team-Building Tips

  • Every party needs a healer. Start with Karaha-Baruha (available at 100 pts). Apururu (UC), Yoran-Oran (UC), and Nashmeira II are solid upgrades as you progress.
  • A tank makes everything safer. August is the strongest tank trust in the game. Rughadjeen and Amchuchu are solid budget picks.
  • Support trusts multiply your damage. Koru-Moru (Haste + Refresh), Ovjang (Haste + Refresh), and Qultada (Corsair rolls) are top-tier.
  • Fill remaining slots with damage. Zeid II, Prishe II, and Gilgamesh are strong melee. Shantotto II and Ullegore nuke well.
  • II and upgraded versions are different, not just stronger. Some II versions trade utility for raw aggression (e.g. Zeid vs Zeid II). Compare the individual pages to pick the right one for the fight.
Scythe Custom — Trust Upgrade System

Level up your trusts through four tiers

Most trusts on Scythe can be individually upgraded from Base → Tier I → Tier II → Tier III through a custom upgrade quest system. Higher tiers unlock new spells, new abilities, and stronger stats on a trust-by-trust basis. For example, Nashmeira II gains Haste, Refresh, Haste II, Phalanx, and Raise across her tiers; tank trusts gain stronger enmity siphon. Upgrades are per-character and per-trust, and carry forward forever.

How to upgrade a trust

  1. Earn Trust Currency. Three types, all stored as character variables (not items): Trust Stones, Trust Gems, Trust Jewels. Exchange Merit Points and Job Points for them at the Trust Stone Exchange NPC in Ru'Lude Gardens:
    • 5 Merit Points = 1 Trust Stone (used for Tier I)
    • 10 Merit Points or 100 Job Points = 1 Trust Gem (used for Tier II)
    • 500 Job Points = 1 Trust Jewel (used for Tier III)
  2. Find the trust's upgrade NPC. Every upgradeable trust has a dedicated NPC scattered across the starting nations, Jeuno, Adoulin, and a few specialty zones. They use the trust's in-game model — look for familiar faces in unusual spots (e.g. Zeid in Bastok Mines, Shantotto in Windurst Walls, Maat in Ru'Lude Gardens).
  3. Talk to them to see the requirements. Each tier asks for a specific combination of: stones/gems/jewels of the right type, a unique trade item (usually a themed piece of job equipment or a signature weapon/armor), and sometimes a condition like nation rank, job level, or kill count.
  4. Trade the item. The NPC consumes the trade item and the stones, advances the trust's tier, and the upgrade takes effect the next time you summon them.

Not every trust is upgradeable — about 54 have the tier system wired in (mostly the popular picks and story characters). The rest function as a single base version. Check individual trust pages below for tier-by-tier details on what each upgrade unlocks.
