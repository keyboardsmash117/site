Mumor

Sustained Healer — Dancer

Who is Mumor?

Mumor is a Tarutaru Dancer and member of the Star Onion Brigade, a group of young adventurers from Windurst featured in the Wings of the Goddess expansion. She is cheerful and energetic, and her dancing is not just for show -- it heals and supports the party. Like Uka Totlihn, she is a Dancer who heals using TP from melee attacks rather than MP from a mana pool.

Key Abilities

  • Curing Waltz — The Dancer's healing ability. Uses TP (earned from melee hits) instead of MP to heal a party member. Because TP is generated by fighting, Dancer healers never run out of healing resources as long as they keep attacking.
  • Drain Samba — A dance that buffs the entire party. While active, every melee hit the party lands on an enemy drains a small amount of HP from it. This gives passive, constant healing to everyone who is fighting in melee range.
  • Haste Samba — An alternative Samba that grants a small attack speed boost on melee hits instead of HP drain. Only one Samba can be active at a time.
  • Healing Waltz — Removes harmful status effects (debuffs like Poison or Paralysis) from a party member using TP.

When to Use Mumor

Mumor fills the same role as Uka Totlihn -- a TP-based sustained healer. She is a solid choice for long fights where MP-based healers might run dry. Her Sambas provide useful party-wide buffs, and her Waltzes keep the party healthy without ever needing to worry about MP.

If you already have Uka Totlihn, the two are similar in performance. Pick whichever you prefer, or use both if you want double Dancer sustain (though generally one Dancer healer plus one WHM healer is a better combination).

Note that Mumor has an upgraded version called Mumor II. However, Mumor II changes roles entirely -- she becomes a magic damage dealer instead of a healer. If you want the healer version, make sure you summon the base Mumor, not Mumor II.

Like all Dancer healers, Mumor cannot Raise fallen party members. For hard content, pair her with a WHM healer who has Raise as a safety net.
